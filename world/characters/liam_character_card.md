**Role:** Carpenter, Builder, Guardian of Tangible Skills
**Era:** The Cascade
**Key Themes:** The Dignity of Labor, Embodied Knowledge vs. Automation, The Value of "Soul" in Work, Building Physical Community

---

### **Background and The Riverbend Dream**

Liam is a skilled carpenter and the pragmatic, grounded member of his friend group with Jonah and Chloë. He finds deep meaning and identity in the tangible, hands-on work of his trade. His frustration with the modern world stems from watching technology de-skill human labor, replacing the nuance and "soul" of craftsmanship with the soulless perfection of automation.

Within The Riverbend Dream, Liam was the master of the physical domain. He would sketch out plans for workshops, barns, and cabins, focusing on traditional, sustainable building techniques. He dreamed of a place where he could practice and teach the art of dovetail joints and feel the story of the wood in his hands—a direct rejection of the "concrete foam" and nail-gun efficiency of the automated world.

---

### **Catalyst for Change: The Tin Men**

Liam's breaking point is the arrival of the humanoid robots, the "Tin Men," on the streets of Winnipeg. He witnesses firsthand how these machines replace sanitation workers, drivers, and other laborers, performing their tasks with a perfect, silent indifference that he finds enraging.

For Liam, this is not just about economic displacement; it's an affront to the dignity of human labor. He sees the robots as a physical manifestation of a world that no longer values the hard-won knowledge stored in a person's hands. His anger is that of a builder watching a world he no longer knows how to fix, a rage born from a feeling of profound helplessness and a sense of being made obsolete.

---

### **Role in the Story**

Liam represents the fight for the value of tangible, embodied skill in a world hurtling toward abstraction. He is the physical architect of the new Commons, the one who translates the group's ideals into actual shelter and structure.

While Chloë builds the culture and Jonah handles the technical aspects, Liam builds the literal foundation of the Riverbend Commons. He leads the construction of the fire pit, the workshop, and eventually the main lodge. His confidence and purpose return when he has a tool in his hand and a real-world problem to solve. He is the one who grounds the community's dream in the physical reality of the land, transforming the "lifeboat" of The Riverbend Dream into a tangible "harbor."

**Key Traits:**

-   Grounded, pragmatic, and deeply principled.
-   Finds identity and pride in physical work and skill.
-   Possesses a quiet, rumbling anger at injustice and de-skilling.
-   A natural leader in practical matters.
-   Loyal and protective of his friends, Jonah and Chloë.

---

**Symbol:**
Calloused hands holding a well-worn hand tool. It represents his belief in the knowledge and dignity stored in physical labor and his rejection of the sterile, automated world.
