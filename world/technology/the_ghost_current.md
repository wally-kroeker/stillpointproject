# The Ghost-Current

**TYPE:** Decentralized Communication Network

## Description

The Ghost-Current is the primary communication network of the Commons, a decentralized, resilient, and radically private mesh network built on open-source principles. It is a hybrid system, blending a low-power, long-range "skip-net" with high-bandwidth local "pools," designed to be functionally invisible to outside surveillance. The network's philosophy is a direct reflection of the Commons themselves: interconnected, self-healing, and fiercely independent.

## Core Concepts & Terminology

The Ghost-Current uses a unique lexicon that blends its technical function with poetic metaphor:

*   **The Current:** The name for the network as a whole, evoking a constant, invisible flow of information.
*   **Skipping:** The act of sending a message or data packet. A user doesn't "send" a message; they "skip" it into the Current.
*   **Stones:** Small, low-power, long-range nodes that form the backbone of the network. Based on LoRa (Long Range) radio technology, these devices are often embedded in actual stones, carved pieces of wood, or other innocuous objects and scattered across the landscape. They relay tiny, efficient packets of data over vast distances, operating "below the noise floor" of conventional network traffic.
*   **Pools:** Local, high-bandwidth Wi-Fi mesh networks that exist within a Commons like [[Geode Commons]]. This is where data from the Current collects, is processed by local AIs like [[Satya's AI Model]], and is accessed by users.
*   **Skippers:** The crucial "Hybrid Bridge Nodes" that connect the Stones to the Pools. A Skipper can be a dedicated piece of hardware at the edge of a Commons or a skilled Tinker who manages the flow of data. They intelligently route traffic, ensuring a message "skipped" from a distant Stone finds its way to the correct Pool.
*   **Cipher-Shroud:** The Ghost-Current's defining security feature. It is an end-to-end, application-layer encryption protocol. Before a message is even "skipped," it is wrapped in a "shroud" of AES-256 encryption. This ensures that the Stones and Skippers that relay the message are only ever handling an opaque, meaningless blob of data. Only the sender and the intended recipient have the keys to remove the shroud, guaranteeing privacy even if a packet is intercepted.

## History and Origins

The roots of the Ghost-Current lie in the pre-Cascade era. Early versions, built on open-source firmware like **Meshtastic**, were used by activists, journalists, and privacy advocates to create ad-hoc communication networks during protests and in regions with heavy corporate or state surveillance. These early "skip-nets" were a lifeline, allowing for encrypted, off-grid communication when centralized networks were compromised or shut down.

During [[The Cascade]], these networks proved their worth a thousand times over. As corporate networks buckled and AIs like the [[Chorus AI Platform]] turned the internet into a tool of control, the skip-nets became the primary means of coordination and survival for the nascent Commons.

Post-Cascade, the first generation of Commons Tinkers formalized these systems, blending the long-range "skip-nets" with local Wi-Fi mesh technology. They codified the principles of open-source hardware and software, radical privacy, and decentralized topology, giving birth to the Ghost-Current as it is known today.

## Philosophy and Principles

*   **Open & Transparent:** The designs for all Stones and Skippers, and the code that runs the Current, are publicly available within the Commons. This transparency is non-negotiable, as it allows anyone to audit the system for vulnerabilities or backdoors, fostering a deep, communal trust.
*   **Resilient by Design:** The Ghost-Current has no central point of failure. Like a real current of water, it flows around obstacles. If a Stone is destroyed or a Skipper goes offline, messages will automatically reroute through the remaining nodes.
*   **Privacy as a Precondition:** The network is not just private; it is designed to be *invisible*. The combination of low-power transmission and the Cipher-Shroud makes its traffic nearly indistinguishable from background radiation, a "ghost" in the machine of global surveillance.
