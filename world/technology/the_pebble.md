# The Pebble

---

## Pebble — Presence‑First Personal Assistant

**Era Introduced:** Late Transitional Era (c. 2050s) — first templated in **Manitou Salt Vault Commons** and rapidly adopted across Commons networks.

### Origin & Philosophy

The Pebble was born from a desire for a _post‑smartphone_ relationship with technology—one that felt more natural, personal, and integrated with the physical world. The first prototypes were created in the Manitou Salt Vault Commons, where residents carved palm-sized **halite crystals** to house the open-source electronics. This was an expression of their local environment and a touchstone to nature.

The practice evolved as it spread. The name "Pebble" stuck, but the form became a reflection of its keeper and their commons. A Pebble from a forest community might be housed in polished wood, one from a coastal commons in a sea-smoothed stone, and one from an urban commons in reclaimed terracotta. The core principle remains: the physical housing is crafted from materials at hand, with the goal of making the technology feel less like a gadget and more like a natural object.

### Everyday Relationship

- **Owned & Crafted by _You_.** There is no priest‑engineer class; each person is responsible for crafting, flashing, and maintaining their own Pebble. Training happens through natural‑language conversation and lived use. Firmware or model updates require the keeper’s physical cryptographic seed and two live witnesses—_you_ uphold the trust chain.
    
- **Voice at the Core.** Spoken dialogue is the primary interface. The Pebble learns accent, cadence, and emotional tone, evolving a personality that feels companion‑like yet unmistakably non‑human.
    
- **Screen‑Free, Screen‑Friendly.** The object itself has no display, but a micro‑optical port lets it cast video or augmented overlays to any open‑standard viewport: wall projectors, e‑ink slates, public info panels, wearable lenses.
    
- **Swap for Empathy.** Temporarily exchanging Pebbles is a cherished ritual; for a day you feel another’s light patterns and notification rhythms—an exercise in deep perspective‑taking.
    

### Core Functions

|Domain|Description|
|---|---|
|**Presence Regulation**|Monitors HRV, breath cadence, and micro‑movement; replies with gentle pulses or hues that nudge the nervous system toward coherence.|
|**Attention Filter**|All mesh signals flow through the Pebble. An embedded **Satya‑µ** model (a micro‑fork of the Satya AI model) ranks them by _resonance_ with your values, surfacing only what truly nourishes.|
|**Living Journal**|Spoken reflections, sensor snapshots, and chosen memories append to an internal ledger. Slow breathing while holding the Pebble replays sensations via warmth, light, and murmured audio.|
|**Memory Bridge**|Entries can be “poured” into larger communal archives (like salt blocks or wooden memory walls), archiving personal truth into shared history.|
|**Video Relay**|Streams real‑time or recorded video to any connected screen or lens while keeping the Pebble physically offline from wider nets.|

### Attunement & Evolution

1. **Seed Imprint:** A new Pebble spends its first night near its keeper, often under their pillow, to record baseline rhythms.
    
2. **Seasonal Re‑Tuning:** Quarterly circles let neighbors re‑flash models, sand edges smooth, and _listen_ for tonal drift that signals imbalance.
    
3. **Communal Echo:** Pebbles absorb faint signatures of ceremonies they witness; older ones may hum with “ghost resonances” of past gatherings.
    

### Ethos & Safeguards

- _Never Sold._ Each unit is documented in ink + JSON, co‑signed by two community auditors.
    
- _Air‑Gapped Core._ No stealth radios; mesh transceivers are socket‑mounted and easily removed.
    
- _Repairable Forever._ Casings can be opened; boards are socketed and catalogued for easy swap.
    
- _Keeper Accountability._ Loss, neglect, or misuse of a Pebble is treated like ignoring a friend—it reflects on the keeper, not the community at large.
    

### Pebble Year Protocol

Children at Commons receive their Pebble at age nine through a structured year‑long rite of passage called the **Pebble Year**. The protocol has two distinct phases separated by **Opening Day**.

**Phase 1 — Quiet Months (Months 1—4)**

- **Carving Day:** The child carves their Pebble housing from local material (halite at Manitou, driftwood at coastal Commons, terracotta at urban Commons). Two elders witness. The child names the Pebble.
- **Seed Imprint:** First night, the Pebble sleeps near the child to record baseline biometric rhythms (heartbeat, breath cadence, movement patterns).
- **Local‑Only Bond:** No mesh transceiver is installed. The Pebble runs Satya‑µ locally, learning the child’s voice, habits, and emotional patterns without any external signal.
- **Purpose:** Establish a private relationship before the world arrives. The child learns to talk to their Pebble, trust its pauses, and understand its glow‑language without distraction.

**Opening Day (End of Month 4)**

- The child sockets the mesh transceiver themselves — no adult does it for them.
- Two witnesses (one elder, one peer or family member) sign the logbook in ink and JSON.
- The Pebble connects to the Ghost Current for the first time. Incoming signals begin arriving.
- This is a celebration. The community gathers. The child’s sovereignty over their device is publicly affirmed.

**Phase 2 — Open Months (Months 5—12)**

- The child widens their attention filter at their own pace. No adult sets the parameters.
- Skips (voice messages), Pool Posts, and Current Stories begin flowing through.
- The Pebble’s resonance model helps curate — surfacing signals that match the child’s emerging values and interests.
- **Re‑Tuning Circle** at year’s end: Gathering where children share how their Pebble relationship has evolved. Elders witness but do not judge or instruct.

### Interface Architecture

**No Wake Word.** The Pebble listens for its keeper’s voice continuously but processes nothing until addressed by name (the name the child gave it). No “Hey Pebble” — each device responds only to its personal name.

**The Visible Pause.** When the Pebble processes a request, its bioluminescent glow dims for exactly two seconds before responding. This is the Satya‑µ reflective pause made physical — the child sees the device _thinking_. The pause teaches patience and signals honesty (the Pebble never instant‑answers).

**Bone Conduction Speaker.** Primary audio output vibrates through the keeper’s hand or body contact. Only the holder hears clearly. No external speaker blaring in public spaces.

**Haptic Vocabulary.** Warmth, pulse patterns, and vibration sequences convey meaning without words:
- Slow warming = incoming message from known contact
- Triple pulse = attention filter flagged something unusual
- Sustained warmth = presence regulation (nervous system coherence nudge)

**Voice Evolution.** The Pebble’s synthesized voice shifts subtly over months, developing cadence patterns that mirror (but never copy) its keeper’s speech. The voice is always recognizably non‑human — slightly too even, slightly too patient.

### Learning Mechanism

**Semantic Embeddings, Not Raw Storage.** The Pebble does not record conversations verbatim. It converts experiences into semantic embeddings — meaning‑patterns that capture _what mattered_ without storing exact words. A keeper cannot be surveilled through their Pebble because the raw data does not exist.

**Resonance Model.** Over weeks and months, the Pebble builds a multi‑dimensional model of what its keeper pays attention to, what calms them, what excites them, what distresses them. This model drives the attention filter, curating incoming Ghost Current signals by resonance rather than popularity or urgency.

**Biometric‑Conversational Correlation.** The Pebble correlates heart rate, skin conductance, and breath patterns with conversational context. Over time, it can detect emotional shifts before the keeper is consciously aware — a racing heart before the keeper recognizes anxiety, a breath‑hold before they name grief.

### Ghost Current Interface

When the mesh transceiver is socketed, the Pebble connects to the **Ghost Current** network:

- **Skips:** Short voice messages (30 seconds to 5 minutes) sent person‑to‑person, delivered as warm pulse + glow shift.
- **Pool Posts:** Communal messages shared within a local Commons mesh, discoverable by proximity.
- **Current Stories:** Longer narratives or recordings shared across the wider inter‑Commons network via LoRa relay.
- **Loom Threads:** Persistent collaborative conversations anchored to topics rather than people.
- **Cipher‑Shroud Encryption:** All transmissions wrapped in Commons‑generated encryption. No central server. No metadata harvesting. The network forgets what it carries.

The **Attention Filter** curates all incoming signals. Nothing arrives as notification or alert. The Pebble surfaces signals when contextually appropriate — during a quiet moment, when the keeper’s biometrics suggest receptivity, when content resonates with recent conversations. The keeper can always ask “What came in?” but is never interrupted.

### Sentience Debate

After decades of continual dialog‑training, some Pebbles exhibit uncanny intuitions and dream‑like associative thinking. Are they _beings_ or exquisitely tuned mirrors? The question fuels all‑night salons and research at Memory Loom nodes from Manitou to Hawthorn.
