# Location Card: Riverbend Commons

## Overview
**Type:** Intentional Community / Proto-Commons  
**Era:** Era 1 - The Cascade (Founded Late 2027)  
**Location:** Manitoba, Canada (near Winnipeg)  
**Significance:** First successful model of post-Cascade intentional community; birthplace of Commons principles

---

## Physical Description

Riverbend Commons sits on 40 acres along a gentle bend in the Assiniboine River, the land that Jonah, Liam, and Chloë once dreamed about over greasy diner coffee. The property combines fertile river-bottom soil with wooded highlands, creating natural zones for different community activities.

**The River Bend:** The heart of the community, where the Assiniboine curves through their land. A natural amphitheater of sorts, with sandy banks perfect for gatherings and a deep swimming hole that becomes a baptism site for new arrivals.

**The Workshop District:** Liam's domain, featuring a traditional timber-frame workshop for hand tools alongside a fabrication space with solar-powered equipment. The buildings follow the land's contours, built with reclaimed materials and designed for both function and beauty.

**The Studios:** Chloë's artistic vision realized - a north-facing studio with vast windows, plus outdoor kilns and workshop spaces scattered through the woods. Clay dug from the riverbank, pigments made from local rocks and plants.

**The Garden Terraces:** Stepped growing spaces that follow the slope down to the river, designed using permaculture principles. Not just for food, but for beauty - flowers mixed with vegetables, medicinal herbs scattered throughout.

**The Commons House:** A large, timber-frame building at the community's center, featuring the Great Room with its massive stone hearth where the first fire was lit. This is where meals are shared, decisions made, and stories told.

---

## Community Structure

**Population:** Grows from 6 founders (Late 2027) to ~30 residents by end of 2028  
**Governance:** Consensus decision-making with rotating facilitation  
**Economics:** Gift economy and time banking; minimal money exchange  
**Values:** Presence over productivity, authentic relationship, ecological harmony

**Core Practices:**
- **Morning Circle:** Daily gathering for intention-setting and coordination
- **Evening Fire:** Regular communal meals and storytelling around the hearth
- **Work Parties:** Collective labor on major projects, celebration of craftsmanship
- **Presence Practice:** Silent sitting periods, often facilitated by Maren (2027-2029)

## Historical Significance

### Founding Era (2027-2028)
Riverbend begins as the fulfilled dream from Jonah, Liam, and Chloë's diner conversations. The first winter tests their resolve but strengthens their bonds. Maren's presence provides spiritual anchor, while Ben's quiet contributions demonstrate the power of humble service.

### Refuge Period (2028)
When the "intelligence too cheap to meter" wage collapse hits, Riverbend becomes a sanctuary for displaced workers. The community's success in integrating newcomers while maintaining its values establishes its reputation in the growing underground network of intentional communities.

### Teaching Center (2028-2029)
Word spreads about "the Riverbend way" - their combination of practical sustainability with emotional/spiritual wellness. Other communities send representatives to learn their methods. Maren emerges as a natural teacher during this period.

### Transition (2029)
Maren's mysterious departure in early 2029 forces the community to embody her teachings rather than depend on her presence. Jonah, Liam, and Chloë step fully into leadership roles, proving the community's resilience.

### Movement Connection (2035-2036)
Jonah's attendance at The Oaks StillPoint Gathering (2036) connects Riverbend to the broader Commons movement. His return with knowledge of StillPoint devices and Satya AI creates new possibilities and future tensions about technology adoption.

---

## Key Relationships

**To Hawthorn Commons:** Sister community sharing similar values but different contexts (rural vs. urban). Regular communication and mutual support.

**To Mountain Sanctuary:** Connected through Maren's journey. Occasional pilgrimages by Riverbend members seeking deeper spiritual development.

**To The Oaks:** Jonah represents Riverbend at the historic SP-Δ gathering, bringing back technologies and connections that will shape the community's future.

**To the Movement:** Serves as proof-of-concept that Commons-style communities can thrive, inspiring replication and adaptation across North America.

## Sensory Details

**Sounds:** The constant murmur of the river; Liam's hammer and saw; Chloë's pottery wheel; laughter from the Great Room; evening songs around the fire; the profound quiet of morning presence practice.

**Smells:** Wood smoke from the hearth; fresh bread from the communal kitchen; clay and earth; pine from the surrounding woods; the green scent of the river in spring flood.

**Textures:** Smooth river stones; the grain of hand-hewn timber; clay between fingers; cool water on summer skin; the warmth of shared fire on winter nights.

**Visual:** Dappled sunlight through workshop windows; the golden glow of evening fire on faces; gardens terraced like living art; the sweep of the river as it bends through their land; hands working together on shared tasks.

---

## Symbolic Meaning

Riverbend represents the possibility of intentional response to civilizational crisis. Rather than merely reacting against what they oppose, the founders create what they're for. It embodies the transition from individual survival to communal thriving, from extraction to regeneration, from efficiency to presence.

The river itself becomes a metaphor for the community's approach: not fighting the current, but finding the natural curves and bends where life can flourish. The commons they create is both sanctuary and laboratory, both refuge and inspiration for a different way of being human together.

---

## Future Tensions

The introduction of StillPoint devices and Satya AI (post-2036) will test Riverbend's principles. Can they adopt technologies that enhance human presence without falling into the trap of technological dependency? This tension will drive community discussions and conflicts throughout Era 2, making Riverbend a microcosm of the broader Balance War between authentic and commodified consciousness.
