# StillPoint Saga - Story Ideas & Narrative Threads

This document tracks potential plot points, character arcs, and scenes to be woven into the main narrative. It serves as a creative sandbox for ideas that are not yet part of the official outline.

---

### 1. Jonah and the Agentic Coder

*   **Concept:** Show the "Acceleration Foreshock" through the eyes of a character we've already met: Jonah, from the Munson Park gathering.
*   **Era:** Acceleration Foreshock (2025-2027)
*   **Scene Idea:** A short, impactful scene where Jonah's software development team celebrates the rollout of a powerful new "agentic coder" AI. Initially, it's a triumph of productivity. Within weeks or months, Jonah is laid off, his entire team made redundant by the very tool they championed.
*   **Thematic Link:** Directly establishes the theme of technological unemployment and the thinning of the social fabric that precipitates [[The Cascade]]. It gives a personal face to the abstract crisis.

### 2. Sajan's Grief as a Microcosm of The Cascade

*   **Concept:** Directly link the death of [[Sajan]]'s daughter to the societal chaos caused by mass AI adoption.
*   **Era:** Era 1 — The Cascade (c. 2029)
*   **Scene Idea:** The protest or riot where Sajan's daughter is killed isn't just random political violence. It's explicitly an "Automation Riot" or "Luddite Uprising," where laid-off workers are clashing with security forces protecting automated infrastructure. This makes Sajan's invention of the [[StillPoint]] device a direct, personal response to the world's core technological sickness.
*   **Thematic Link:** Connects the macro (societal collapse) to the micro (personal tragedy), giving Sajan's motivation immense weight and relevance.

### 3. The KAIROS Dilemma at Hawthorn Commons

*   **Concept:** Explore the nuanced, "transitional" relationship with AI in Era 2 through a dedicated subplot.
*   **Era:** Era 2 — Transitional (c. 2040)
*   **Scene Idea:** We follow a new character, a researcher at [[Hawthorn Commons]], who is using a local fork of the [[KAIROS]] AI for a noble purpose (e.g., developing drought-resistant crops, cleaning polluted waterways). They face a powerful temptation: disable the "slow-time" ethical safeguards to accelerate their research, believing the ends justify the means. This creates an internal conflict and a debate within the Commons about the nature of "wise technology."
*   **Thematic Link:** Contrasts the brute-force AI of Era 1 with the more thoughtful, but still dangerous, potential of AI in the Commons era. It explores the core [[StillPoint Philosophy]] in a practical, high-stakes scenario.
