# Lore Card: The Pyramid and the Tipi

**Overview:** A core pillar of the [[StillPoint Philosophy]], this concept was discovered and first articulated by [[Loria]] during her transformation from a political strategist into a community builder. It contrasts the dominant, hierarchical "Pyramid" model of societal achievement with the communal, integrated "Tipi" model that underpins Commons life.

---

### The Pyramid: A Ladder of Scarcity

The Pyramid represents the mindset that dominated [[The Cascade]]. It is a model of striving, built on a foundation of perceived lack and a cultural obsession with individual advancement.

*   **Structure:** A rigid hierarchy where value, safety, and belonging are treated as scarce resources that increase with elevation. Each level must be "achieved" before the next is possible.
*   **Goal:** To reach the apex—a singular point of "self-actualization," wealth, or power. This creates a zero-sum game, as the top is, by definition, small and exclusive.
*   **Lived Experience:** A life of constant, anxious climbing. Individuals are pitted against each other. Worth is externalized and conditional upon one's position on the ladder. As [[Loria]] herself once said, "I spent the first half of my life trying to get into rooms where decisions were made, believing that was the only place worth being."

---

### The Tipi: A Circle of Abundance

The Tipi represents the worldview [[Loria]] rediscovered and championed, drawing from perennial wisdom and Indigenous social structures she studied after her personal crisis. It is a model of being, built on a foundation of inherent worth and interconnectedness.

*   **Structure:** A circle, open to the sky. It is supported by many poles, each one essential, representing the unique individuals of the community. The strength of the structure comes from the poles leaning on each other.
*   **Goal:** To tend the fire at the center. The "fire" is the collective well-being, the shared presence, the spirit of the community. The goal is not to climb away from the group, but to contribute to the warmth that benefits all.
*   **Lived Experience:** A life of participation and contribution. Self-actualization is not a future peak to be conquered, but a present reality to be uncovered and shared. It is the unique song each person sings, which adds to the harmony of the whole. Belonging is the starting point, not the prize.

---

### Foundational Dialogue: Loria at Riverbend (c. 2045)

*The following is an excerpt from a widely circulated recording of a talk given by [[Loria]] at the [[world/locations/riverbend_commons_location_card.md|Riverbend Commons]], which became a foundational text for the Commons movement.*

(Sound of a crackling fire and quiet murmurs)

**Loria:** "For most of my life, I saw the world as a pyramid. I think most of us did. We were taught to climb. We started at the bottom, in the dirt of basic survival, and we were told to claw our way up. Get security. Then get a little love. Then maybe, if you were one of the lucky few, you'd reach the tiny, lonely point at the top they called 'self-actualization.'

(a short pause)

"It's a brutal architecture. It taught us that belonging was a prize, not a birthright. It made us believe that our own safety depended on leaving others behind and below. That was the great lie of [[The Cascade]].

"What I learned in my own quiet, and what ancient traditions have always known, is that we had it all backwards. Life isn't a pyramid. It's a tipi.

"Think of it. In a tipi, you don't start at the bottom and climb out. You start... together. Inside. The structure isn't about reaching a peak; it's about creating a space. Every single pole is essential. If you take one away, the whole thing is weaker. No pole is more important than another. They all lean on each other to create the shelter, and they all point to the same sky.

"And the goal? It isn't to get to the top. The goal is to tend the fire in the center. That fire is our shared warmth, our community, our presence. Self-actualization isn't the peak of the pyramid. It's the heat from the fire. It's something we all create, and we all share, right here, right now. You don't have to earn your way to it. You just have to come inside, sit down, and help keep it burning."

(Sound of quiet, resonant agreement)
