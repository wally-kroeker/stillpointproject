---
title: "Night of Dance (Transitional Era)"
---

Evening settled over the Sanctuary as people trickled in, shrugging off city noise and the weight of their day. The gathering space was alive but relaxed—soft mats and cushions scattered in a wide circle, some pressed against old painted walls, others tucked near the base of the big server rack at the center. The rack itself was nothing fancy: just an old computer tower patched together from recycled gear, stickers, and notes from past gatherings. Lights—some LED strips, some paper lanterns—draped overhead, shifting color in time with the music.

No one made a big announcement. As the playlist rolled out, people started to move—some swaying in place, some stretching out on mats, a few diving into full-bodied dance. The StillPoint’s speakers adjusted on their own: deepening the bass, then letting it fade, matching the pace and mood in the room. Lights flickered gently, sometimes wild and bright when the group’s energy peaked, sometimes almost invisible as everything slowed.

By the end, even the ones who hadn’t danced were breathing easier. There was a hush—a sense of shared arrival. Mara, arms wrapped around her knees, caught herself smiling at the way the room felt like one soft, breathing thing.
