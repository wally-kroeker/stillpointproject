# Era 1 Outline Draft (Revised v3)

## Part 2: The Cascade (2028 - 2032)

**Core Theme:** The struggle to cultivate "living stillness" in a small community while the "dead noise" of the outside world reaches a fever pitch. The dream of the commons is tested by harsh reality.

**Protagonist Arc:** Jonah and the Riverbend founders move from idealistic escape to the gritty work of building a resilient harbor. Introduction of Sajan's parallel journey, born from grief.

---

**Chapter 8: The First Winter (Riverbend, Early 2028)**
*   **Summary:** The small community at Riverbend (Jonah, Liam, Chloë, and a few others) struggles through their first winter. Resources are scarce. The outside world's "psychic static" bleeds in through news feeds—market crashes, wage collapses. Their dream is tested by the harsh reality of survival. They must decide on their core principles for sharing, governance, and dealing with the fear that they've made a terrible mistake.
*   **World Bible Tie-in:** Dramatizes the 2028 event: "Public trust erodes as 'intelligence too cheap to meter' crashes market wages." Shows the micro-level impact on a fledgling community.
*   **Thematic Touch-Stone:** [[The Pyramid and the Tipi|world/lore/the_pyramid_and_the_tipi.md]] (internal conflict over leadership and resource allocation).

**Chapter 9: A Knock at the Door (Riverbend, Late 2028)**
*   **Summary:** A family, displaced by the economic crash, arrives at Riverbend seeking refuge. They are not idealists, just desperate. This forces the founders to confront the reality of their "lifeboat." Is it just for them, or is it a harbor for others? They decide to take the family in, stretching their resources and testing their ideals.
*   **World Bible Tie-in:** Shows the practical beginnings of the Commons movement as a response to crisis, not just ideology.
*   **Thematic Touch-Stone:** Voluntary, non-coercive social change.

**Chapter 10: The Inauguration (Elsewhere, Jan 2029)**
*   **Summary:** A short, sharp chapter cutting away from Riverbend. We see news feeds and fragmented scenes of the political chaos as a constitutional crisis leads to a third Trump term. Riots flare, and Steadfast militias become a visible presence. The tone is jarring and chaotic, contrasting sharply with the quiet struggles at the Commons and raising the external stakes dramatically.
*   **World Bible Tie-in:** Dramatizes the Jan 2029 event: "Constitutional loophole → Trump 3rd term; riots & Steadfast militias flare."
*   **Thematic Touch-Stone:** Living Stillness vs. Dead Noise.

**Chapter 11: The Grief Engine (Palo Alto, March 2029)**
*   **Summary:** We meet **[[Sajan|world/characters/sajan_character_card.md]]**. We see him not as a physicist, but as a father shattered by the death of his daughter, [[Satya Mehta|world/characters/satya_mehta_character_card.md]], killed in a protest. Consumed by grief, he retreats to his lab. He's not trying to save the world; he's trying to find a place of quiet where his pain can simply *be*. He begins tinkering with quantum sensors, trying to build a device that can detect the "silence" he craves. This is the birth of the **SP-0**.
*   **World Bible Tie-in:** Dramatizes the March 2029 event: "Sajan’s daughter, Satya Mehta, killed; SP-0 StillPoint prototype born of grief."
*   **Thematic Touch-Stone:** Grief transmuted into communal creativity.

**Chapter 12: The Tin Men (Riverbend, 2030)**
*   **Summary:** The outside world intrudes again. A fleet of [[Tin Men|still_point_world_bible.md:67]] arrives to perform infrastructure maintenance on a nearby power line. They are efficient, silent, and utterly inhuman. Their presence is a constant, unnerving reminder of the world Jonah and the others left behind. It also creates a new kind of tension, as local authorities managed by [[Chorus|world/technology/chorus_ai_platform.md]] see Riverbend as an unregistered, inefficient anomaly.
*   **World Bible Tie-in:** Introduces the [[Tin Men|still_point_world_bible.md:67]] and the increasing pressure from the [[Chorus|world/technology/chorus_ai_platform.md]]-managed state.
*   **Thematic Touch-Stone:** Presence vs. distraction.

**Chapter 13: The Resting Place (Minneapolis, 2032)**
*   **Summary:** News travels through the [[Ghost-Current|world/technology/the_ghost_current.md]] network that a major hub has formed in a reclaimed Minneapolis megachurch, simply called the "Resting Place." Jonah and Chloë travel there and witness a thriving, organized community. They don't meet a single leader, but they hear stories about a quiet, steady presence within the community—a person named **Maren**—who seems to be a source of its stability. They leave inspired by the model, with the name "Maren" echoing as a symbol of what's possible.
*   **World Bible Tie-in:** Dramatizes the 2032 event. Connects Riverbend to a larger movement and introduces Maren as a mysterious, influential figure.
*   **Thematic Touch-Stone:** Spiral history: crisis → integration → stillness.

## Part 3: The Counter-Current (2033 - 2036)

**Core Theme:** The transition from isolated pockets of resistance to a networked, self-aware movement. The birth of the technology that will define the era.

**Protagonist Arc:** Jonah finds a new purpose, lending his technical skills to a larger cause. Sajan reluctantly steps into the light, sharing his private creation with the world.

---

**Chapter 14: The Festival (The Oaks, 2035)**
*   **Summary:** We meet **[[Gray Tesluk|world/characters/gray_tesluk_character_card.md]]**, a brilliant generative artist whose complex, AI-driven light show is the centerpiece of The Oaks festival. Sajan, now a nomadic figure, is convinced to bring his strange "shoebox" device (the SP-0). He gives a demo. While most are intrigued, Gray sees the missing piece for their art: a real-time, biological data source. They realize they can make the lights respond not to the music, but to the audience's collective nervous system. This insight sparks the collaboration that will define the next year.
*   **World Bible Tie-in:** Dramatizes the 2035 event. Introduces [[Gray Tesluk|world/characters/gray_tesluk_character_card.md]] and sets up the creation of the SP-Δ.
*   **Thematic Touch-Stone:** Radical transparency & DIY tech ethics.

**Chapter 15: The Guild (Minneapolis & The Ghost-Current, 2035-2036)**
*   **Summary:** Following the festival, Sajan stays in the Twin Cities area, and Gray becomes his lead collaborator. An open-source guild forms around them on the [[Ghost-Current|world/technology/the_ghost_current.md]]. Jonah contributes remotely from Riverbend, but the core architectural work of integrating Sajan's sensor into a communal system is led by Gray. We see a montage of this Tipi-style innovation, with Gray architecting the system that will become the **SP-Δ**.
*   **World Bible Tie-in:** Shows the formation of the open-source builder guild with Gray as a key architect, per their character card.
*   **Thematic Touch-Stone:** [[The Pyramid and the Tipi|world/lore/the_pyramid_and_the_tipi.md]].

**Chapter 16: The Gathering (The Oaks, Summer 2036)**
*   **Summary:** The climax of Era 1. The guild, with Gray and Sajan at the center, brings the completed **SP-Δ** back to The Oaks. Thousands attend. Jonah is there, witnessing the culmination of the work. The device is activated, and Gray's light systems respond to the crowd's deepening stillness, creating a stunning visual feedback loop for the "unstruck note." The movement is no longer a fringe idea; it's a force.
*   **World Bible Tie-in:** Dramatizes the Summer 2036 event. Highlights Gray's central role in the technology.
*   **Thematic Touch-Stone:** The 'unstruck note': rediscovering the universal presence.

**Chapter 17: The Sprouting (Across the region, Late 2036)**
*   **Summary:** The final chapter of Era 1. In the wake of The Gathering, new Commons sprout. We see Riverbend continue as a small, tight-knit community—a single, valid model of living. In contrast, **[[Hawthorn Commons|world/locations/hawthorn_commons_origins_and_setting.md]]** begins to form around the Minneapolis Resting Place as a sprawling, beautiful "patchwork." It's a network of diverse groups—religious circles, tech co-ops, artist collectives—all bound by a shared principle of mutual aid and allowing individuals to thrive and be fully themselves, not in service to a hierarchy. The era ends with this contrast, showing there is no single right way to build a commons.
*   **World Bible Tie-in:** Dramatizes the Late 2036 event. Clearly defines the difference between Riverbend and the "patchwork" of Hawthorn, incorporating the new thematic depth.
*   **Thematic Touch-Stone:** Voluntary, non-coercive social change.
