### Chapter 1: The Efficiency Win
- [x] **Scene 1:** `novel/scenes/e1_c01_s01_the_daydream.md` (Canon)
    - **Core Tension:** Can the trio's long-held, deeply researched dream of a different life survive the immediate, crushing reality of the technologically-driven world they inhabit?
    - **Emotional Arc:** From the warm, familiar ritual of shared dreaming -> to the cold, isolating anxiety of the corporate world -> to the final, chilling realization that the dream is now in peril.
    - **Sensory Palette:**
        - *Diner:* The clatter of ceramic plates, the smell of greasy food and burnt coffee, the sticky vinyl of the booth, the comfortable rhythm of a conversation they've had for years.
        - *Office:* The oppressive, sterile silence punctuated by the subliminal chime of Chorus, the cold blue light of monitors, the feeling of recycled air, the phantom tension in Jonah's shoulders.
    - **Key Beats:**
        1. **The Ritual:** The scene opens with Liam and Chloë discussing the latest incursions of soulless automation into their respective fields. This is a familiar conversation, a ritual of shared discontent.
        2. **The Catalyst:** Jonah spots a classified ad for rural property, continuing their long-standing game of finding potential locations for "the project."
        3. **Naming the Dream:** They riff on their well-worn plans, referencing their annual camping trips and Chloë's research. Liam plans his workshop, Chloë her studio, and Jonah his blend of tech and self-sufficiency. Chloë reaffirms the name they've given this dream: "Riverbend."
        4. **The Interruption:** A Chorus notification on Jonah's phone shatters the moment, pulling him back to his corporate reality.
        5. **The Efficiency Win:** Back at the office, Jonah watches as the AI he trained flawlessly and inhumanly fast completes his team's project, making him obsolete.
        6. **The Dream in Peril:** The scene ends with Jonah staring at the screen, his hands useless. The memory of their shared, deeply-researched dream flashes in his mind—not as a comforting hope, but as a tangible, cherished thing that the machine has just attacked.
    - **Thematic Resonance:** This revised scene establishes the trio's bond and their dream of Riverbend as a long-standing, well-researched project, not a fleeting fantasy. This deepens the stakes and makes the eventual loss and transformation of this dream more profound.

## Part 2: The Birth of the StillPoint (2029 - 2036)

### Chapter 8: The Strategist
- [ ] **Scene 1:** Key Elements: Introduce Loria, her mastery of Chorus, the psychic static it causes, and the global context of the Third Term protests and the China/Taiwan AI chip conflict. She sets the Boston protest in motion.

### Chapter 9: The Physicist
- [ ] **Scene 1:** Key Elements: Introduce Sajan and his idealistic teenage daughter, Satya. Their loving but tense relationship is established, centered on her desire to join the protests.

### Chapter 10: The Cost of a Win
- [ ] **Scene 1:** Key Elements: The protest spirals into a riot. Satya is killed. The chapter cuts between Loria's detached screen-based view, Sajan's frantic search, and Satya's terrifying experience on the ground.

### Chapter 11: The Grief Group
- [ ] **Scene 1:** Key Elements: Sajan, broken by grief, joins a support group for parents who also lost children. He brings the first prototype, the SP-0 "Bio-Harmonic Mirror," and the group experiences a moment of shared, technologically-assisted peace.

### Chapter 12: The First LogBook
- [ ] **Scene 1:** Key Elements: A member of the grief group suggests commercializing the SP-0. Sajan is horrified and, in response, creates the first LogBook, establishing the device's open-source, gift-economy principles.

### Chapter 13: The Nomad's Song
- [ ] **Scene 1:** Key Elements: Sajan's "nomad year" in his van. He witnesses ancient forms of communal healing (music, dance, breath) and realizes his device is a "tuning fork" for these, not a replacement. This insight leads him to The Oaks festival.

### Chapter 14: The Oaks '35
- [ ] **Scene 1:** Key Elements: Sajan's first, quiet visit to the festival. He shares the SP-0 from his van, attracting a small group of open-source makers. The idea for a crowd-scale SP-Δ is born around a campfire.

### Chapter 15: The Mountain Sanctuary
- [ ] **Scene 1:** Key Elements: We return to Loria, who has fled her old life. Under the mentorship of Elder Maren, she studies wisdom traditions and begins to formulate the philosophical language of the infinite game as an antidote to the Chorus mindset.

### Chapter 16: The Convergence of Minds
- [ ] **Scene 1:** Key Elements: Loria tracks Sajan down to the Minneapolis maker's guild. Their multi-day meeting weaves her philosophical framework with his quantum science, creating the unified Stillpoint Philosophy. She helps them draft a charter for the open-source Satya AI.

### Chapter 17: The StillPoint Gathering
- [ ] **Scene 1:** Key Elements: The triumphant return to The Oaks in '36 with the completed SP-Δ. The legendary demonstration of mass coherence becomes the spark for the wider movement and Jonah's future journey.
